Falafel recipe

Description

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground chickpeas blended with fresh herbs, garlic, onions, and aromatic spices. The mixture is shaped into small patties or balls and deep-fried until crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

Falafel is commonly served in pita bread with fresh vegetables, pickles, and tahini sauce. It is a flavorful vegetarian meal that is enjoyed as breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Directions

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and pat them dry.
  2. Add the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, and spices to a food processor.
  3. Pulse until the mixture is finely chopped but not completely smooth.
  4. Mix in the baking soda and let the mixture rest for about 30 minutes.
  5. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  6. Heat the oil to 350°F (175°C) and fry the falafel until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve warm with pita bread, salad, tahini sauce, and pickles.
